## Domain Knowledge
- Empirical compounds group isotopologues (e.g., m+13C3) and in-source adducts (e.g., [M+H]+, [M+Na]+) that represent the same chemical entity and co-elute chromatographically.
- Default khipu tolerances are 5 ppm mass accuracy and 2 seconds retention time window; tighter tolerances reduce false-positive grouping of unrelated features.
- Charge states up to z=3 and common solvent/chromatography-dependent adducts (e.g., [M+HCOO]− in negative ESI) must be pre-specified or inferred from ionization mode.
- Singleton features (not clustered with any isotopologues or adducts) cannot receive Level 4 formula annotations because their adduct state is ambiguous.
