## Research Question
How does the blank_masking command filter features from a metabolomics feature table based on the intensity ratio between unknown samples and blank samples?

## Connected Finding
The blank_masking command removes features by comparing feature intensity in study samples (designated by sample_value) to intensity in blanks (designated by blank_value) using a configurable blank_intensity_ratio parameter; features whose intensity in unknown samples is not at least the specified ratio times more than blank samples are dropped.

## Domain Knowledge
- Blank masking assumes that blank samples are reasonably aligned with study samples in terms of chromatographic and instrumental conditions; misalignment may require adjusting the intensity ratio threshold or performing separate masking for different blank types (e.g., process blanks vs. solvent blanks).
- The intensity ratio is computed excluding zero values to avoid division errors and to focus on genuinely detected features; features completely absent in blanks or unknowns require special handling.
- Default blank_intensity_ratio of 3 means unknown sample intensity must be ≥3-fold the blank intensity; different ionization modes, chromatographic modes, or sample preparation protocols may require empirical optimization of this threshold.
- Blank masking should typically be one of the first processing steps after feature extraction, before normalization or batch correction, to ensure downstream steps work only with true biological/chemical signals.
- Multiple blank types (e.g., process blanks, solvent blanks, extraction blanks) may require iterative masking steps with potentially different thresholds applied sequentially to achieve effective contamination removal.
