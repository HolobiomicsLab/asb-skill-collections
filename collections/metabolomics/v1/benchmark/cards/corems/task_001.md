## Domain Knowledge
- Hanning apodization is a windowing function applied to time-domain FT-ICR transients before FFT to reduce spectral leakage; zero-filling (padding with zeros) increases frequency-domain resolution.
- Mass-domain calibration uses reference compounds of known exact mass to establish a calibration curve that corrects systematic mass errors across the spectrum.
- CHO elemental constraints (carbon, hydrogen, oxygen only) restrict the molecular formula search space for small organic molecules, reducing false positive assignments.
- Mass error is typically expressed in parts-per-million (ppm) and represents the difference between measured m/z and theoretical m/z; smaller absolute error indicates better formula confidence.
- CoreMS MSParameters encapsulation allows parametrized configuration of the entire processing pipeline including apodization, noise threshold, calibration tolerance, and formula search settings.
