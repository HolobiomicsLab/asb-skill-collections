## Domain Knowledge
- Retention indices (RI) are normalized retention times calculated relative to a series of alkane standards (typically n-alkanes C10–C40) and are more reproducible than absolute retention times across GC-MS instruments and columns.
- Low-resolution GC-MS mass spectra typically have m/z resolution ≤1 Da and must be matched against library spectra using cosine similarity or similar dot-product metrics that are robust to intensity normalization differences.
- NIST and PNNL spectral libraries encode both mass spectral fragmentation patterns and experimental retention indices, enabling two-dimensional compound matching that improves specificity over mass-spectrum-only searches.
- ReadAndiNetCDF parses Agilent .d (ChemStation) and Thermo .raw formats converted to NetCDF; it extracts instrument metadata, detector response traces, and scan-level mass spectra for downstream processing.
- False-positive identifications are minimized by applying combined thresholds on both spectral match score (typically ≥700 on a 0–1000 scale) and retention-index deviation (typically ≤20 RI units from library value).
