## Domain Knowledge
- PPM (parts per million) tolerance is a relative mass accuracy metric critical for matching observed m/z peaks against reference standards; typical starting windows range from 2–10 ppm depending on instrument resolution.
- A minimum of 5 calibration points is a standard quality threshold for reliable polynomial mass-to-m/z conversion, as fewer points risk overfitting and poor extrapolation.
- SRFA (Suwannee River Fulvic Acid) is a reference compound standard widely used in Fourier-transform mass spectrometry (FTMS) calibration due to its well-characterized and stable m/z peaks.
- Calibration residuals quantify the fit error between observed m/z values and the polynomial model, and systematic high residuals indicate poor calibration quality or reference-peak misidentification.
