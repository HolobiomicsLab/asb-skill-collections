## Domain Knowledge
- Over-representation Analysis (ORA) in metabolomics requires careful definition of the statistical background to avoid bias and spurious pathway enrichment.
- The background set must be comprehensive (all metabolites measured or measurable in the study) while excluding the experimental detection list to ensure valid statistical testing.
- Pathway database quality and coverage directly affect background-set composition; missing or incomplete pathway annotations can distort enrichment p-values.
- ORA assumes conditional independence between pathways given the background; pathways with zero or very few background metabolites compromise the validity of enrichment tests.
