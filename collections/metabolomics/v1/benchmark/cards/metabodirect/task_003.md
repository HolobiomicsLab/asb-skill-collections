## Domain Knowledge
- PERMANOVA tests the null hypothesis of no difference in multivariate metabolite composition between groups using permutation tests; a non-significant result (p > 0.05) indicates no detectable difference in exometabolite profile between phage types HP1, HS2, and uninfected control.
- NMDS is a rank-based ordination method that preserves pairwise distances between samples in low-dimensional space; it is robust to non-normal data and works with any dissimilarity metric, making it ideal for count-like or intensity-based metabolomics data.
- Bray-Curtis distance is abundance-weighted and appropriate for metabolite intensity data; Jaccard distance is presence-absence only; Euclidean distance is sensitive to magnitude but assumes linearity; choice affects PERMANOVA power and NMDS configuration.
- FT-ICR MS peak intensities reflect relative abundance without absolute quantification; normalization (sum-normalization or other methods) is required before PERMANOVA to account for total ion current variation between samples.
- Viral infection of bacteria can reprogram host central metabolism, altering exometabolite composition; a non-significant PERMANOVA result suggests that phage type (podovirus HP1 vs. siphovirus HS2) does not drive measurable shifts in the exometabolome under nutrient-rich (P-replete) conditions.
