## Domain Knowledge
- SMILES (Simplified Molecular Input Line Entry System) is a linear notation for encoding chemical structures and must be translated to a canonical form to enable deduplication and downstream analysis.
- Chemical sanitization removes structurally invalid, incomplete, or malformed SMILES entries before inclusion in curated datasets.
- Deduplication is critical to prevent redundant structure-organism associations and to maintain data integrity across 42,166 unique organisms and 231,330+ curated structures.
- The LOTUS database aggregates data from 31 initial open databases, requiring standardisation of diverse SMILES representations to a single canonical form.
