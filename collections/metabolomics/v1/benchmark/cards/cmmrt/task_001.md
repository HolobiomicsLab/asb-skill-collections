Configure a deeply regularized DNN architecture with heavy regularization (L1/L2 penalties) and train using cosine annealing with warm restarts as the learning rate scheduler. 5. Apply stochastic weight averaging during training to improve generalization. 6. Evaluate trained model on held-out test set and compute mean absolute error (MAE) and median absolute error (MdAE) with reported uncertainties (±1.2 s and ±0.9 s respectively).

## Domain Knowledge
- Cosine annealing with warm restarts (SGDR) cycles the learning rate between high and low values across epochs to escape local minima and improve DNN generalization for regression tasks.
- Stochastic weight averaging (SWA) averages model weights across multiple epochs with low constant learning rates to find flatter minima and reduce test error without hyperparameter tuning.
- Molecular fingerprints (MACCS166, Extended Connectivity, Path-based) encode chemical substructure patterns as binary vectors and typically outperform high-dimensional descriptor sets for QSAR and retention time prediction.
- Heavy regularization (L1/L2 penalties, dropout) is essential when training DNNs on chemical datasets to prevent overfitting on the 80,038-sample SMRT dataset and stabilize predictions across chemical space.
- Retention time prediction error is reported as both mean absolute error (robustness across the full distribution) and median absolute error (robustness to outliers); both metrics with ±std reflect cross-validation uncertainty.
