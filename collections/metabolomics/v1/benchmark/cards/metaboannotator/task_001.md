## Domain Knowledge
- MetaboAnnotatoR performs all-ion fragmentation (AIF) metabolite annotation by matching observed fragment ions against curated ion fragment databases such as LipidPos, ranking candidates by cosine similarity or related matching scores.
- The annotateRC function requires three synchronized data objects: an xcms peak table (peak m/z, retention time, and intensity), a RAMClustR object containing pseudo-MS/MS spectra (clustered fragment ions per feature), and a specified fragment library.
- LipidPos is a lipid-specific fragment ion database; using it biases results toward lipid hits and may exclude non-lipid metabolites even if they match better in other libraries.
- Rank-1 annotation status means the candidate ranked highest by the matching algorithm; this does not guarantee correctness without manual verification of matched ion patterns.
- The reported benchmark result (three of six features annotated, LPC(14:0) rank-1 for feature 3) serves as the reproducibility target and should be matched exactly by the annotateRC run.
