## Domain Knowledge
- CCS bias represents systematic deviation between measured and theoretical collision cross section values caused by instrumental calibration drift or ESI-source conditions in IM-MS.
- The DTCCSN2 library uses universally labeled U13C lipid standards to establish reference CCS values, enabling internal standardization without requiring external calibration beyond vendor specifications.
- Effective CCS bias assessment requires matching experimental lipid features to the reference library by both mass accuracy and chromatographic retention behavior; false matches inflate bias estimates.
- CCS bias is typically expressed and aggregated at the lipid class level (e.g., PC, PE, TAG) because systematic bias patterns are often class-specific due to differences in ionization efficiency and charge distribution.
