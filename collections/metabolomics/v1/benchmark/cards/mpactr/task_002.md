## Domain Knowledge
- Mispicked ions are isotopic patterns incorrectly split during peak-picking that can be identified by similarity in retention time and mass and merged using sum aggregation.
- Solvent and media blanks are negative controls whose feature abundances above a relative threshold (e.g., 0.01 or 1%) should be removed to eliminate carryover and background contamination from experimental samples.
- Coefficient of variation (CV) threshold of 0.2 (20%) is used to identify and flag non-reproducible compounds between technical replicates; ions exceeding this threshold indicate inconsistent detection.
- The qc_summary() function returns a structured record of each input compound's fate across all applied filters, enabling visualization and interpretation of which quality control step removed which features.
