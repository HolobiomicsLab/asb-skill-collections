## Domain Knowledge
- Isotopologue grouping relies on precise mass differences corresponding to stable isotopes: C13 (~1.003 Da), N15 (~0.997 Da), O18 (~2.004 Da), and D (~1.006 Da).
- Common adduct masses must account for ionization mode: [M+H]+ (1.008 Da), [M+Na]+ (22.989 Da), [M+K]+ (38.963 Da), [M+NH4]+ (18.034 Da) in positive mode; [M−H]− (−1.008 Da), [M+Cl]− (34.969 Da) in negative mode.
- Retention time clustering is essential to group features of the same molecule across adduct variants, as they elute at the same or very similar times due to identical chromatographic behavior.
- Mass accuracy tolerance (typically ≤5 ppm) determines the allowable deviation from theoretical mass differences when linking isotopologues and adducts.
