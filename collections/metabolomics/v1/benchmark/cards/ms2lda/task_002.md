## Domain Knowledge
- LDA hyperparameters (alpha controls topic sparsity, beta controls topic-word sparsity) must be tuned for spectral fragmentation patterns; typical alpha is document-length-normalized, beta is uniform or learned.
- Mass2Motifs represent latent topics of co-occurring fragment ions and neutral losses; each motif is a probability distribution over all possible m/z values observed in the corpus.
- Convergence is typically assessed via the LDA log-likelihood or perplexity on held-out data; models are considered converged when objective stabilizes across iterations.
- The bag-of-fragments representation treats each spectrum as an unordered collection of fragment masses and neutral losses, losing spatial/temporal peak information but enabling topic modeling.
- Serialization to JSON and pickle ensures portability: pickle preserves the full LDA state for downstream inference, while JSON makes motifsets human-readable and compatible with visualization and MotifDB comparison.
