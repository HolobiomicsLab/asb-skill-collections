## Domain Knowledge
- RSDQC (Relative Standard Deviation of QC samples) is computed on batch-corrected compound/internal standard ratios and measures consistency of ionization and detector response across repeated QC injections, with lower values indicating more stable internal standards.
- An internal standard is optimal for a given compound when its RSDQC is minimized, indicating that the compound-to-standard ratio exhibits the least variability after batch correction.
- Batch correction using pooled study quality control samples (SQC) adjusts for instrument drift and matrix effects; RSDQC must be calculated post-correction to reflect true analytical stability.
- The exhaustive evaluation across all internal standard candidates per compound acknowledges that no single internal standard performs equally well for all analytes due to differential ionization suppression and detector response.
