Execute env.run() to drive the simulation loop and capture all scans with exclusion-weighted acquisition decisions. 6. Call env.write_mzML() to serialize the scan list to mzML format. 7. Load the pickled EvaluationData object and compute coverage and intensity metrics using evaluate_simulated_env(), comparing times_fragmented_summary and cumulative intensity against the baseline TopN result.

## Domain Knowledge
- WeightedDEWController implements dynamic exclusion with weighted scoring to improve acquisition of lower-intensity ions by down-weighting previously fragmented precursors, contrasting with standard TopN strategy that always selects the top N most intense ions.
- EvaluationData pickle files store the complete trace of chemicals, generated scans, and fragmentation events necessary to compute metrics like fragmentation times and cumulative intensity coverage without re-running the simulation.
- The times_fragmented_summary metric counts how many times each chemical was selected for MS2 fragmentation, with differences between WeightedDEW and TopN reflecting improved sampling breadth versus intensity-biased acquisition.
- Isolation window (1 m/z), m/z tolerance (10 ppm), and RT tolerance (15 s) determine dynamic exclusion boundaries; m/z_tol is used for matching precursor ions across scans to decide whether to exclude or fragment a candidate ion.
- The min_ms1_intensity threshold (1.75E5) filters which precursor ions are eligible for fragmentation, simulating typical instrument sensitivity thresholds; WeightedDEW may achieve better coverage by fractionally lowering this threshold for previously under-sampled m/z regions.
