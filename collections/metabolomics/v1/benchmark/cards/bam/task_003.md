## Domain Knowledge
- Global molecular networking links MS features with high spectral similarity to infer shared structural motifs or metabolite families, enabling collective annotation.
- Biotransformation rules define in silico predicted phase I and phase II metabolic transformations; candidate structures from these rules must be mapped back to experimental features via mass-to-charge and spectral matching.
- Spectral similarity thresholds (e.g., cosine similarity >0.6–0.8) are critical for filtering false-positive edges in the network and determining network resolution.
- Annotation confidence depends on both spectral similarity to known spectra and consistency with biotransformation rules; features with multiple candidate structures require scoring or ranking.
- Network visualization and interpretation require understanding of community structure (clusters), node centrality, and bridge edges that connect distinct molecular families.
