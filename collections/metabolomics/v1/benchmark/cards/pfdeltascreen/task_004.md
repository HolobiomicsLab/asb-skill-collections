## Research Question
How does PFΔScreen detect and flag potential PFAS features by identifying diagnostic fragments and characteristic mass differences in MS2 spectral data?

## Domain Knowledge
- PFAS diagnostic fragments are characteristic product ions (e.g., CF₃⁻, CF₂⁻, SO₂⁻) that appear in MS2 spectra and serve as biomarkers for PFAS compound classes.
- Mass tolerance (typically 5–10 ppm in high-resolution HRMS) determines whether an observed product ion mass matches a known diagnostic fragment within instrumental accuracy.
- Characteristic mass differences (e.g., loss of functional groups) between precursor and product ions provide additional diagnostic signatures beyond exact fragment masses.
- Centroided MS2 spectra have peaks represented as m/z–intensity pairs rather than profile mode raw data, enabling direct mass matching against fragment databases.
