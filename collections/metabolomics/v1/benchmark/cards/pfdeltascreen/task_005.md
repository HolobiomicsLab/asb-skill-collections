## Research Question
Can PFΔScreen's prioritization filters (MD/C-m/C, KMD analysis, and MS2 fragment diagnostics) correctly process and filter PFAS features supplied via an optional custom feature list, rather than relying solely on pyOpenMS-detected features?

## Domain Knowledge
- MD/C (mass defect / molecular mass ratio) is a key discriminator for PFAS identification because fluorine incorporation creates characteristic mass defects that differentiate PFAS from isomeric non-fluorinated compounds.
- Kendrick mass defect analysis clusters PFAS homologues by molecular weight spacing (typically 2 × 13.007825 Da per CF₂ unit) and is most effective when features share a common fluorinated backbone structure.
- Custom feature lists bypass automated pyOpenMS peak detection and must supply exact m/z values, retention times, and intensities to ensure compatibility with downstream mass-based filters that assume feature-level precision.
- MS2 diagnostic fragments (e.g., CF₃⁻, CF₂⁻, or SO₂NH⁻ for PFOS-like compounds) are structure-specific and their presence/absence in the MS2 data is used to confirm or reject candidate PFAS features identified by mass-based filters alone.
