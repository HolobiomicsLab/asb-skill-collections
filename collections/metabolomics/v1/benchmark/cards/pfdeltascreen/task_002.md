## Workflow Description
1. Load feature list with m/z values and assigned molecular formulas. 2. Calculate exact mass for each molecular formula using standard atomic masses. 3. Compute mass defect (observed m/z minus exact mass) for each feature. 4. Divide mass defect by the number of carbon atoms in the molecular formula to obtain MD/C value. 5. Apply MD/C threshold criterion to flag features as potential PFAS. 6. Output filtered feature list with MD/C scores and PFAS prioritization flags.

## Domain Knowledge
- Mass defect is the difference between observed m/z and the exact mass calculated from a molecular formula; normalized by carbon count, it serves as a signature characteristic of perfluorinated and polyfluorinated compounds.
- PFAS compounds typically exhibit distinctive mass defect per carbon (MD/C) values due to the high mass of fluorine atoms relative to hydrogen substitution.
- Molecular formula assignment requires high-resolution accurate mass data (typically ≤5 ppm error) to reliably compute mass defect.
- The MD/C approach is one of multiple orthogonal prioritization strategies in PFΔScreen; it does not require MS/MS data and operates solely on intact feature masses and formula assignments.
