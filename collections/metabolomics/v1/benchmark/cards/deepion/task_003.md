## Domain Knowledge
- Contrastive learning maximizes agreement between representations of augmented views of the same sample while pushing apart representations from different samples, preventing the encoder from learning trivial constant solutions.
- A Projection module typically maps high-dimensional encoder outputs to a lower-dimensional space via a learnable MLP, making the contrastive loss computation more effective and stable.
- A Prediction module adds an additional MLP head after projection, creating asymmetry between the two branches (stop-gradient on one path) to prevent trivial convergence where the network learns identical representations for all inputs.
- For ion image data in mass spectrometry, preventing representation collapse is critical because collapsed embeddings lose discriminative power needed to distinguish co-localized ions (COL mode) or isotope relationships (ISO mode).
- Contrastive loss functions (e.g., NT-Xent, SimCLR) require careful temperature scaling and batch composition to balance positive pair attraction against negative pair repulsion without dominating gradients.
