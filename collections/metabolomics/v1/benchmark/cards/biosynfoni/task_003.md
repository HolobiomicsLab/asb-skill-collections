## Domain Knowledge
- Bit-frequency in molecular fingerprints measures how often each bit position is set to 1 across all vectors in a dataset, indicating the prevalence of specific chemical substructures.
- Sparsity quantifies the proportion of zero bits in a fingerprint vector; natural product fingerprints often exhibit high sparsity due to the rarity of certain chemical features.
- Tanimoto similarity (Jaccard index) is the standard metric for comparing binary fingerprints, computed as the intersection of set bits divided by the union of set bits, ranging from 0 (dissimilar) to 1 (identical).
- The pairwise Tanimoto similarity distribution across a large fingerprint dataset reveals the chemical diversity and clustering properties of the molecular space represented.
