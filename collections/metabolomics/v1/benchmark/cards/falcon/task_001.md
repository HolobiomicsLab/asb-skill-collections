## Domain Knowledge
- Feature hashing is a dimensionality-reduction technique that converts sparse, high-dimensional spectral data (m/z bins) into fixed-size dense or sparse vectors via hash functions, enabling efficient similarity searching without storing the original spectral resolution.
- Spectral binning discretizes the continuous m/z range into discrete bins; the choice of bin width affects the trade-off between spectral resolution and computational efficiency.
- Low-dimensional hashed vectors enable fast approximate nearest-neighbor search algorithms (e.g., locality-sensitive hashing, LSH) that scale to millions of spectra without exhaustive pairwise comparisons.
- Intensity normalization (e.g., TIC, L2-norm) is essential before hashing to ensure comparable vector representations across spectra with different total intensities.
