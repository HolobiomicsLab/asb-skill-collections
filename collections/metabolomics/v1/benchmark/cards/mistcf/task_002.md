## Domain Knowledge
- The MIST-CF subformula assignment component operates without SIRIUS fragmentation trees, instead using a learned neural network that assigns subformulas to peaks based on precursor formula constraints and observed m/z differences.
- Subformula predictions must satisfy mass-balance constraints: the sum of fragment subformula masses must account for observed peak m/z and ionization mode (positive mode with multiple adduct types including [M+H]+).
- Ranking metrics for subformula assignment evaluate whether the ground-truth label appears in the top-k predictions and penalize incorrect or missing chemical formulas at each peak position.
- The formula transformer architecture learns data-dependent patterns rather than relying on fixed fragmentation rules, allowing prediction across diverse molecule classes without database queries.
