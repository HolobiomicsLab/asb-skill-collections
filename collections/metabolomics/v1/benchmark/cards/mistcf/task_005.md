## Research Question
Can the MIST-CF formula transformer architecture be extended to support negative-mode adducts, and what is the resulting top-k formula ranking accuracy on negative-mode MS/MS spectra?

## Connected Finding
MIST-CF currently considers multiple adduct types beyond [M+H]+ but is still restricted to positive mode only.

## Task Description

## Domain Knowledge
- Negative-mode ionization produces characteristic adducts ([M-H]−, [M+Cl]−, [M+FA]−) with distinct m/z shifts that differ fundamentally from positive-mode adducts and require separate training to be recognized by neural models.
- Formula transformers learn data-dependent fragmentation patterns rather than relying on physicochemical fragmentation rules, so negative-mode spectra exhibit different fragmentation signatures requiring domain-specific retraining.
- Top-k accuracy (top-1, top-5, top-10) is the standard ranking metric for de novo chemical formula assignment and must be computed on a held-out test set that is independent of the fine-tuning set.
- Sinusoidal formula embeddings (SCARF) encode molecular structure as continuous vectors; this representation is mode-agnostic and need not be retrained, only the adduct-aware output layers.
- Public MS/MS repositories (MassIVE, MetaboLights) require stringent filtering for high-quality negative-mode entries with unambiguous formula and adduct annotations to avoid training-set contamination.
