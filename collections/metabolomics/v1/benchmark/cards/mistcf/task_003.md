## Research Question
What is the performance improvement in chemical formula ranking accuracy when MIST-CF incorporates support for multiple adduct types compared to restricting predictions to [M+H]+ only?

## Domain Knowledge
- MIST-CF uses an energy-based transformer neural network that ranks formulas without relying on fragmentation-tree databases, making it suitable for de novo formula inference from MS/MS data.
- The [M+H]+ adduct is the most common positive-mode ionization but represents only one of multiple possible adduct types; multi-adduct support expands the search space and may improve or degrade ranking depending on data quality and model calibration.
- Formula-ranking accuracy is typically measured as top-1 (exact match of formula and adduct at rank 1) and top-k (correct formula-adduct pair within top-k predictions), where k is often 5 or 10.
- Ablation studies isolating individual model components (like MULTI_ADDUCT_SUPPORT) require identical experimental conditions (same dataset, same hyperparameters, same inference procedure) except for the single toggled feature.
