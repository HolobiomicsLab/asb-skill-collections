## Research Question
How can sodium adduct ions be visually distinguished from their parent ions in mass spectrometry imaging data?

## Connected Finding
The pointsAdducts() function generates a scatter plot that highlights adduct ions in red and parent ions in contrasting colors, enabling visual identification of the red/blue overlap pattern between sodium adducts and their corresponding parent ions.

## Task Description
Generate a scatter plot annotation of the mass spectrum highlighting sodium adduct ions (red points) and their parent ions (blue outlines) from a correlation-filtered, adduct-matched massdiff object, showing the red/blue overlap pattern where some adduct ions serve as parents for further adducts.

## Domain Knowledge
- In MALDI-MSI, sodium adducts form when sodium ions attach to neutral molecules during ionization, producing peaks at mass m/z = [M+Na]+ with the characteristic mass difference of 22.99 Da from the parent molecular ion.
- Spatial correlation testing in MSI exploits pixel-level intensity covariance between parent and putative adduct peaks; high Pearson correlation coefficients (after Bonferroni multiple-testing correction) indicate that the two peaks co-occur spatially, supporting a true adduct relationship rather than chance mass-difference matching.
- The overlap of red and blue annotation points on a mass spectrum indicates molecular ions that serve a dual role: they are simultaneously the product adduct of a lighter parent ion AND the parent ion for a heavier adduct, creating a chain of related molecular species.
- The pointsAdducts() function requires prior annotation of peak pairs with adductMatch() and significance filtering via corrPairsMSI(); calling it without this preprocessing will result in empty or misleading visualizations.
- Visual inspection of the annotated spectrum is a key validation step: the expected red/blue overlap pattern confirms that the workflow correctly identified ions with multiple adduct relationships in the same biological sample.
