## Domain Knowledge
- Adducts form when target molecules bind with matrix or salt ions during MSI sample preparation or ionization, creating ion pairs with characteristic mass differences that are spatially co-localized in tissue imaging.
- Pearson correlation of ion intensity profiles across pixels tests whether parent and putative adduct ions show consistent spatial co-variation, which is biological evidence that they derive from the same molecular species.
- Bonferroni correction multiplies the p-value threshold by the number of tests performed to control false-positive rate across thousands of ion pairs; only p-values below threshold/(total pairs) are deemed significant.
- Mass resolution varies with m/z in most mass spectrometers, so histogram binning with fixed-width bins is less accurate than correlation-based matching for identifying specific adducts; the latter is preferred for detailed analysis.
