## Research Question
How are MS2 peaks and neutral losses counted and aggregated from a set of MS2 spectra to produce a per-sample MS2 fingerprint vector?

## Connected Finding
MS2 fingerprints are generated by counting the occurrence of MS2 peaks and neutral losses (relative to the precursor ion) in each sample, producing a numeric fingerprint representation.

## Workflow Description
1. Load MS2 spectra files using matchms to parse fragmentation data. 2. Iterate through spectra in each sample and extract precursor m/z values. 3. Count occurrences of all MS2 peaks (m/z values above noise threshold) in each spectrum. 4. Calculate neutral losses by subtracting observed fragment m/z from precursor m/z for each peak. 5. Count occurrences of all unique neutral losses per sample. 6. Aggregate peak and neutral loss counts across all spectra within each sample using spec2vec document conversion. 7. Generate a numeric fingerprint vector per sample combining peak and neutral loss occurrence counts, normalized by sample size.

## Domain Knowledge
- MS2 fingerprinting counts the raw occurrence frequency of fragment m/z values and neutral losses (precursor m/z minus fragment m/z) rather than their intensity or abundance.
- Neutral losses are calculated by subtracting each observed fragment m/z from the precursor ion m/z of the parent spectrum.
- The fingerprint vector aggregates peak and neutral loss counts across all spectra belonging to a single sample, creating a sample-level rather than spectrum-level representation.
- The MS2 fingerprint approach is retention-time agnostic, making it suitable for comparing samples acquired under different LC or instrument conditions without chromatographic alignment.
