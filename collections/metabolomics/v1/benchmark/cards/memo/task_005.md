## Domain Knowledge
- MDS and PCoA are complementary ordination methods that reduce high-dimensional distance matrices to 2D/3D coordinates for exploratory sample visualization in metabolomics.
- MS2 fingerprints represent sample-level counts of fragment peaks and neutral losses, and pairwise distances between samples are typically computed using cosine similarity, Jaccard, or Euclidean metrics.
- MDS preserves global distance structure while PCoA explicitly captures variance along orthogonal axes (eigenvectors); choice depends on whether maximizing variance or preserving pairwise distances is the research goal.
- MEMO's RT-agnostic alignment allows comparison of chemodiverse samples or those with strong retention-time drift across different LC methods or instrument platforms (e.g., MaXis Q-ToF vs Q-Exactive).
