- External calibration requires measurement of concentration standards across a known range to establish the relationship between MS intensity and analyte concentration.
- Linear or polynomial regression models are commonly used to fit calibration curves; model selection depends on the dynamic range and linearity of the instrument response.
- Calibration quality is assessed via R² fit metrics and residual analysis to ensure the model reliably predicts unknown sample concentrations.
- The calibration component operates independently of drift correction; it assumes either drift-corrected intensity data as input or that drift is negligible over the measurement batch.
