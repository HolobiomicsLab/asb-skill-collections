## Domain Knowledge
- Spectrum normalisation standardises intensity values across spectra to enable fair similarity comparison between query and library spectra of different absolute intensities.
- Peak filtering removes noise and low-intensity m/z signals that do not contribute to spectral identity and can increase false-positive matches in library search.
- Pre-processing must preserve the relative abundance patterns that define spectral identity for MS/MS analogue matching.
- MS/MS spectral data consists of precursor m/z and fragment ion m/z-intensity pairs, where intensity normalisation is critical for reproducible cosine similarity calculation.
