## Background

MS2DeepScore addresses a key challenge in metabolomics: predicting structural similarity between chemical compounds from mass spectrometry data alone, without requiring known chemical structures. The authors trained a Siamese neural network on 109,734 MS/MS spectra from 15,062 unique compounds to predict Tanimoto scores based on molecular fingerprints. On a held-out test set of 3,601 spectra from 500 unseen compounds, MS2DeepScore achieved root mean squared error of approximately 0.15 without uncertainty filtering and 0.1 when applying interquartile range thresholds (IQR < 0.025) to remove high-uncertainty predictions. The model demonstrates superior precision and recall compared to modified Cosine and Spec2Vec when identifying structurally related compounds (Tanimoto > 0.6) across the full range of similarity thresholds. Monte-Carlo Dropout ensemble learning further improves predictions and provides uncertainty estimates, reducing average RMSE from 0.17 to 0.11 with most significant improvements in extreme Tanimoto score ranges. The spectral embeddings generated by the network encode chemically meaningful features, as demonstrated by consistent clustering of molecules from the same chemical superclass in t-SNE visualizations. MS2DeepScore operates on peak m/z positions and intensities alone without requiring spectral metadata, making it applicable to diverse MS/MS datasets.

## Research questions

- Does the MS2DeepScore model trained on RDKit Daylight fingerprints achieve the reported prediction accuracy (RMSE ~0.15 without uncertainty filtering, ~0.10 with IQR < 0.025 filtering) when run on the held-out test set of 3,601 spectra from 500 unique compounds?
- Does MS2DeepScore outperform classical spectral similarity measures (modified Cosine) and the unsupervised Spec2Vec method in retrieving chemically related compound pairs from mass spectral datasets?
- Does applying Monte-Carlo Dropout ensemble filtering (IQR < 0.025) to MS2DeepScore predictions significantly reduce root mean squared error across different Tanimoto score ranges, particularly in low (< 0.4) and high (> 0.8) bins?
- How does the Siamese neural network base network convert a binned MS/MS spectrum into a 200-dimensional spectral embedding?
- Do the 200-dimensional spectral embeddings learned by MS2DeepScore encode chemically meaningful information that enables molecules of the same chemical class to cluster together in low-dimensional space?
