## Background

Reproduces 3 reported results: MEMO generates MS2 fingerprints by counting the occurrence of MS2 peaks and neutral losses in each sample, which are then aligned to compare different samples. MEMO generates MS2 fingerprints by counting the occurrence of MS2 peaks and neutral losses in each sample, which serve as the basis for sample comparison and alignment. MEMO enables alignment of MS2 fingerprints generated from separate samples to compare them in a second stage. Reconstructs 2 described mechanisms (described in the paper but not separately evaluated there): MS2 fingerprints are generated by counting the occurrence of MS2 peaks and neutral losses (relative to the precursor ion) in each sample, producing a numeric fingerprint representation. MEMO applies MDS/PCoA visualization techniques to aligned MS2 fingerprints to generate low-dimensional embeddings that enable visual comparison of different samples.

## Research questions

- How are MS2 peaks and neutral losses counted and aggregated from a set of MS2 spectra to produce a per-sample MS2 fingerprint vector?
- Does the memo_from_unaligned component correctly generate a MemoMatrix object with the expected structure and content when applied to unaligned MS2 spectra files?
- Does the memo_from_aligned component correctly generate a MemoMatrix with the expected structure and content when applied to aligned feature tables with associated MS2 spectra?
- What is the output format and structure when two separately generated MemoMatrix objects are merged using the merged_memo() function?
- How do MDS or PCoA visualization techniques operate on MS2 fingerprint data to produce low-dimensional sample comparison plots?

## Methods overview

Parse MS2 spectra files using matchms to extract precursor m/z and fragment peaks. Count occurrences of all detected m/z peaks within each sample across all constituent spectra. Calculate neutral losses by subtracting fragment m/z from precursor m/z for each peak. Count occurrences of all unique neutral losses within each sample. Aggregate and combine peak and neutral loss counts into a single numeric fingerprint vector per sample using spec2vec document representation. Validation: fingerprint vectors must have non-zero counts for peaks and losses present in the sample and dimensionality consistent with the union of all observed peaks and losses across the sample set. Load and parse unaligned MS2 spectra files using matchms to extract fragmentation peaks and precursor masses. Count occurrences of MS2 peaks and neutral losses (mass differences to precursor) within each sample to construct MS2 fingerprints. Invoke memo_from_unaligned to align fingerprints across samples and generate the MemoMatrix object. Validate matrix structure: verify sample-by-feature dimensionality, presence of metadata attributes, and consistency with unit test expectations.
