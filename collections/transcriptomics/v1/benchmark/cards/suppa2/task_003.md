## Domain Knowledge
- PSI (percent-spliced-in) ranges from 0 to 1 and represents the inclusion ratio of an exon or splice variant across samples.
- ΔPSI is the difference in mean PSI between two conditions; effect sizes near 0.1 are typically considered modest biological changes in splicing.
- SUPPA2's uncertainty-aware approach accounts for expression-dependent variance in PSI estimates to avoid false positives in low-abundance transcripts.
- Transcript expression quantification (TPM, FPKM, or counts) must be provided to weight or filter low-confidence PSI values.
