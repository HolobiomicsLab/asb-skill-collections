## Domain Knowledge
- The lmFit function in limma fits ordinary least-squares linear models to microarray or RNA-seq log-expression data row-wise, with each gene treated as a separate response variable.
- The design matrix must have full column rank and should encode all experimental conditions, batch effects, or covariates of interest to ensure identifiable coefficient estimates.
- Empirical Bayesian methods in limma stabilize variance estimates across genes when the number of arrays is small, improving downstream statistical power in differential expression testing.
- The MArrayLM object output by lmFit contains coefficients, standard errors, and residuals that serve as inputs to empirical Bayes functions such as eBayes for shrinkage and moderated t-tests.
