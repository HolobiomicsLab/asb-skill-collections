## Research Question
How does limma estimate hyperparameters of the prior distribution over gene-wise variances to compute moderated t-statistics and B-statistics?

## Connected Finding

## Domain Knowledge
- Empirical Bayes methods in limma stabilize variance estimates by borrowing information across genes, yielding stable moderated t-statistics even with small numbers of biological replicates.
- The B-statistic represents the log-odds ratio that a gene is differentially expressed versus non-differentially expressed under the prior distribution estimated from data.
- Moderated t-statistics incorporate estimated prior degrees of freedom, shrinking gene-specific variances toward a pooled estimate and reducing false positives from low-variance genes.
- The eBayes function fits a scaled inverse-chi-squared prior to the residual variances from lmFit, using method-of-moments or maximum-likelihood estimation depending on the specified trend argument.
