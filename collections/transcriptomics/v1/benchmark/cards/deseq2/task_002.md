## Domain Knowledge
- Log fold change shrinkage uses data-driven prior distributions to improve effect size estimation, particularly reducing noise from low-count genes without requiring arbitrary filtering thresholds.
- The apeglm estimator uses an adaptive t-prior and is the default as of DESeq2 version 1.28.0; it requires ~5 seconds for typical datasets (~10,000 genes, 7 samples) and can be accelerated 10-fold with apeMethod='nbinomC' at the cost of losing posterior standard deviation estimates.
- The normal estimator uses an adaptive normal prior centered at zero, which can sometimes produce overly strong shrinkage for certain datasets; ashr uses a mixture of normals to provide adaptive shrinkage with potentially less aggressive smoothing.
- Shrunken log fold changes are recommended for visualization and ranking of genes because they remove noise associated with low-count genes, improving interpretability of MA-plots and gene-level summaries.
