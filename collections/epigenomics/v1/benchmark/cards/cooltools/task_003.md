## Domain Knowledge
- Insulation scores quantify local contact enrichment at genomic boundaries and are calculated as the ratio of contacts across a window to contacts within the window.
- Boundary thresholding using Li or Otsu methods converts continuous insulation scores into boolean boundary annotations; Li and Otsu are automatic threshold-selection algorithms from image processing.
- The cooler format stores Hi-C contact matrices as sparse, HDF5-backed data structures with indexed bin tables containing genomic coordinates and optional balancing weights.
- BED (Browser Extensible Data) format requires tab-separated chrom, chromStart, chromEnd columns; cooler bins use 0-based half-open intervals.
- Insulation scores typically increase with genomic distance from strong boundaries and may contain NaN values at chromosome edges or near unmappable regions.
