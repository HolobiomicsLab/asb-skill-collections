## Domain Knowledge
- Eigenvectors from eigenvector decomposition represent the dominant compartmentalization pattern (A/B compartments or multi-compartment continuum) and must be digitized into discrete bins to define which genome regions belong to each compartment category.
- Saddledata is a 2D aggregated contact frequency matrix with rows and columns indexed by the digitized compartment bins, showing how contacts distribute across compartment pairs and revealing compartment interaction preferences.
- Saddle strength is a scalar metric quantifying the degree of asymmetry in off-diagonal saddle blocks, indicating the strength of preferential A–A and B–B over A–B interactions; NaN handling is critical for sparse or low-contact regions.
- The cooler format stores balanced contact matrices using sparse representation; loading and querying requires knowledge of cooler's bin indexing, weight column naming, and masking of bad bins (low-coverage or artefactual regions).
- Digitization thresholds (e.g., quantile-based splitting into quintiles or binary A/B classification) must match the eigenvector distribution and downstream interpretation; fixed vs. adaptive thresholding affects compartment assignment sensitivity.
