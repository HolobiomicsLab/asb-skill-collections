## Domain Knowledge
- Tn5 transposase exhibits sequence-dependent insertion bias that skews ATAC-seq signal at true chromatin accessibility sites and must be corrected before footprinting analysis.
- ATAC-seq footprints are identified as depletions in Tn5 insertion density around transcription factor binding sites; bias correction removes confounding signal from enzyme preference rather than protein occupancy.
- The ATACorrect module models insertion bias from the observed BAM distribution and applies position-weight correction to normalize signal across genomic regions.
- BigWig format enables efficient storage and visualization of genome-wide signal tracks in genome browsers and is the standard input for downstream footprinting scoring algorithms.
