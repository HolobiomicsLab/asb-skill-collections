## Domain Knowledge
- Paired-insertion counting quantifies chromatin accessibility by counting DNA fragment insertions at both ends, differing from insertion-based counting which counts single insertion sites and fragment-based counting which counts whole fragments.
- Tile-based matrices partition the genome into fixed-size intervals (typically 5 kb) for efficient large-scale analysis, enabling scalability to millions of cells.
- Backed AnnData objects store data on disk rather than in memory, enabling analysis of datasets larger than available RAM; fragment data is stored in .obsm['fragment_paired'] for paired-end data or .obsm['fragment_single'] for single-end data.
- The counting_strategy parameter in pp.add_tile_matrix was introduced in Release 2.6.0 to allow flexible choice between insertion-based, fragment-based, and paired-insertion strategies for feature quantification.
