## Domain Knowledge
- Synergy is defined as excess variability of chromatin accessibility for peaks sharing both motifs compared to a random subsample of peaks with one motif (the one with higher variability), quantified as a z-score.
- Correlation between deviations for two annotations can reveal whether cooperative or competitive binding occurs between transcription factors, or instead reflects co-expression or opposing expression patterns.
- The getAnnotationSynergy function is computationally slow and should only be performed on a limited selection of motif pairs, not the full collection of annotations.
- chromVAR's bias-corrected deviations and z-scores normalize for GC content and average accessibility to isolate true biological signal from technical artifacts.
- Low variability and highly correlated annotations should be removed prior to computing correlation to avoid inflated redundancy signals and reduce computational burden.
