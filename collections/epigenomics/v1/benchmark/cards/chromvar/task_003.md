## Domain Knowledge
- K-mer size trade-off: 6-mers are computationally faster but capture less sequence specificity; 7-mers yield higher variability and better de novo motif assembly but require more computation.
- Bias correction in chromVAR accounts for GC content and average accessibility patterns; background peak sets matched on these properties normalize deviation scores to prevent confounding.
- Deviation Z-scores combine effect size (deviation magnitude) with confidence (informed by read depth); higher absolute Z-scores indicate greater departure from expected accessibility, not necessarily higher raw variability.
- Variability is computed as the standard deviation of Z-scores across all cells/samples for a given annotation set; this metric reflects consistency of accessibility patterns and is used to rank motifs or kmers by biological importance.
