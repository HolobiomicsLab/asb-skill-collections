## Domain Knowledge
- Overdispersion in methylation data arises from biological variation beyond sampling variance; the MN (Methylation-specific Negative Binomial) method accounts for this heterogeneity to produce more conservative p-value and q-value estimates.
- The methylBase object is a unified data structure representing methylation calls across multiple samples at the same genomic bases, with coverage and methylation percentage per base.
- Q-values (adjusted p-values) control false discovery rate; more stringent correction results in higher q-values for the same raw test statistic, reducing false positives at the cost of statistical power.
- The Chi-square test is appropriate for testing differential methylation when sample sizes or site counts are sufficient; it tests whether the observed methylation proportion differences are statistically significant.
- dataSim() generates synthetic methylation data with realistic properties (coverage, methylation ratios, replicate structure) to allow reproducible testing and validation of differential methylation workflows.
